## The short answer

Yes. There's nothing in Australian law that requires a will to be handwritten, typed by a lawyer, or produced in any particular way. What matters is that the will meets the legal requirements for validity.

## What online will services provide

A good online will service:

- Asks you the right questions to capture your wishes
- Generates a properly formatted legal document
- Provides clear instructions for signing and witnessing
- Uses templates reviewed by lawyers
- Includes guidance on what to do next

The technology is just a tool for creating the document. The legal validity comes from following the proper signing process.

## When online wills work well

Online wills are well-suited for:

- Straightforward situations (spouse, children, clear beneficiaries)
- People who understand their assets and wishes
- Those comfortable using technology
- Situations without complex family dynamics

## When you might need more help

Consider a lawyer if you have:

- Blended families with competing interests
- Business ownership or complex structures
- Assets in multiple countries
- Family members who might contest the will
- Significant wealth requiring tax planning
- Special needs beneficiaries
