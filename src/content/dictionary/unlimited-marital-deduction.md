<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>A provision of federal tax law permitting unlimited transfers of property between spouses during life or at death without incurring federal estate or gift tax, regardless of amount. The deduction applies only to transfers to citizen spouses; transfers to non-citizen spouses have limitations and may require qualified domestic trusts.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>The unlimited marital deduction means you can give or leave any amount of money or property to your spouse without paying federal estate or gift tax. Married couples don't pay these taxes on transfers between each other, no matter how much.</p>
  </div>
</div>
