<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>A person who has not yet reached the age of legal majority (adulthood) in their jurisdiction. Minors generally cannot make legally binding contracts, make a will, or vote. Age of majority is 18 in most jurisdictions.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>Someone under 18 (or the adult age in your area). Minors can't make wills, sign contracts, or do most legal things adults can. Parents or guardians make decisions for them and manage property left to them.</p>
  </div>
</div>

  <h3>‚è± When you'll encounter this term</h3>

    <li>Appointing guardians for minor children</li>
    <li>Managing inheritance for someone under 18</li>
    <li>Estate planning with young beneficiaries</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div class="example-box">
  <div class="example-label">EXAMPLE</div>
  <p>"My will leaves $50,000 to my nephew, but since he's a minor, it will be held in trust until he turns 18."</p>
</div>
