<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>A will that includes contingent provisions specifying alternative beneficiaries if the primary beneficiaries predecease the testator. Essential for avoiding partial intestacy when first-choice beneficiaries cannot inherit.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>A well-drafted will with backup plans. It says who gets your stuff if your first choice isn't alive. For example: "Leave everything to my wife, but if she's gone, then to my kids."</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="example-box">
  <div class="example-label">EXAMPLE</div>
  <p>"My contingent will leaves my house to my daughter, but if she doesn't survive me, then it goes to her children instead."</p>
</div>

<div class="did-you-know">
  <h3>üí° Did you know?</h3>
  <p>Every well-drafted will should include contingent provisions. Without them, if your primary beneficiary dies before you, that portion of your estate may pass under intestacy laws instead of to people you'd choose.</p>
