<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>A tentative trust created when a depositor opens a bank account in their own name as trustee for another person. The depositor retains full control during life, can withdraw funds or revoke the arrangement, but at death any remaining funds pass directly to the named beneficiary outside probate. Also called a "payable on death" (POD) account.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>A Totten trust is basically a bank account you control completely while alive, but that automatically goes to someone you name when you die. It's the original "payable on death" account‚Äîsimple way to pass money without probate.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="did-you-know">
  <h3>üí° Did you know?</h3>
  <p>Totten trusts are named after the 1904 New York case Matter of Totten, which established that these informal arrangements are valid. Though the term "Totten trust" is less common now, the concept lives on in POD (payable on death) and ITF (in trust for) bank accounts.</p>
