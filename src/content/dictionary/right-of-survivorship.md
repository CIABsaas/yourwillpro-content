<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>A characteristic of joint tenancy whereby, upon the death of one joint tenant, that person's interest in the property automatically passes to the surviving joint tenant(s) by operation of law, outside of probate and regardless of will provisions. The right of survivorship takes precedence over testamentary dispositions.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>Right of survivorship means when one owner of jointly-held property dies, the survivor automatically becomes the sole owner. It doesn't matter what the deceased person's will says‚Äîthe property goes directly to the surviving joint owner, bypassing probate entirely.</p>
  </div>
</div>

  <h3>üí° Why this matters</h3>
