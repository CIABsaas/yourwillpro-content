<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>A specialized court with jurisdiction over the administration of deceased persons' estates, validation of wills, appointment of executors and administrators, guardianship matters, and sometimes conservatorships. Also called Surrogate's Court, Orphans' Court, or Chancery Court depending on jurisdiction.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>Probate court is the specific court that deals with dead people's estates. When someone dies and their will needs to be approved, or when someone needs to be appointed to manage an estate, you go to probate court. It's also where guardianship cases for incapacitated adults and minors are handled.</p>
  </div>
</div>
