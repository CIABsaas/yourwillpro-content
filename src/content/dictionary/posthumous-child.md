<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>A child born after the death of one or both parents. Traditionally referred to children conceived before death but born afterwards. With modern reproductive technology, can include children conceived and born well after a parent's death. Posthumous children have inheritance rights under intestacy laws and may have rights to claim under a parent's will even if not specifically mentioned.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>A baby born after one of their parents has died. Traditionally meant a child conceived before death but born afterwards (like a baby born 3 months after their father died). With IVF and frozen embryos, children can now be born years after a parent's death. These children have the same inheritance rights as children born during the parent's lifetime, though timing can create complications if the estate is already being administered.</p>
  </div>

Different jurisdictions have different rules about whether these children have inheritance rights, especially if conceived after death. Some require the deceased to have consented in writing to posthumous reproduction and inheritance.</p>
