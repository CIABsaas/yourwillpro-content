<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>The formal rejection or disclaimer of a right, inheritance, or fiduciary appointment. A beneficiary may renounce an inheritance, or an executor may renounce their appointment. Renunciation must typically be in writing and filed within specified timeframes, and generally cannot be revoked once made.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>Renunciation is formally saying "no thanks" to an inheritance or a role you've been given. If you're named as executor but don't want the job, you can renounce. If you're left money but want it to go to someone else (like your kids), you can renounce your inheritance.</p>
  </div>
</div>

  <h3>‚è± When you'll encounter this term</h3>

<div class="example-box">
  <div class="example-label">EXAMPLE</div>
  <p>"Mum was named executor of her elderly aunt's estate, but she's 75 herself and didn't feel capable of handling it. She filed a renunciation with the court, and my uncle was appointed as executor instead."</p>
