<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>A provision in a will stating that if a beneficiary challenges the will's validity, they forfeit their inheritance. Also called an in terrorem clause or forfeiture clause. Enforceability varies by jurisdiction‚Äîsome enforce them strictly, some only in limited circumstances, some not at all.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>A clause that says "If you challenge this will, you get nothing." Designed to discourage will contests by threatening to disinherit challengers. Whether courts enforce these clauses depends on your jurisdiction: generally unenforceable in Australia and UK, varies widely in the US. Even where enforceable, they're often ineffective‚Äîthe challenger must be left enough in the will to make the threat meaningful.</p>
  </div>
