<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>Two distinct meanings: (1) The person who authorizes another (an agent or attorney-in-fact) to act on their behalf, typically through a power of attorney; or (2) The capital or corpus of a trust or estate‚Äîthe original assets as opposed to income, interest, or gains generated by those assets. Different rules often govern distribution of principal versus income.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p><strong>Meaning 1</strong>: If you give someone power of attorney, YOU are the principal‚Äîthe person granting the authority. <strong>Meaning 2</strong>: In a trust, the principal is the main pot of money or assets (also called the corpus). If you put $100,000 in a trust and it earns $5,000 in interest, the $100,000 is the principal and the $5,000 is income.</p>

<div class="did-you-know">
  <h3>üí° Did you know?</h3>
  <p>The distinction between principal and income matters enormously in trusts. A common arrangement: the surviving spouse gets all income for life (to support themselves), but the principal is preserved for children to inherit when the spouse dies. This balances the spouse's need for income with protecting children's inheritance, but can create tension if the spouse needs capital for major expenses.</p>
