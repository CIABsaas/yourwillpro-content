<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>A method of distributing an estate where property is divided equally among all living beneficiaries at the same generational level, with each person receiving an equal share "by head" regardless of which family branch they come from. If a beneficiary has predeceased, their share is reallocated among surviving beneficiaries at that level, not passed to descendants. Contrasts with per stirpes.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>Per capita (Latin for "by head") means everyone alive at the same level gets an equal share, ignoring family branches. If your will says "to my grandchildren, per capita," each living grandchild gets an equal share‚Äîit doesn't matter if some grandchildren have siblings or which child they're descended from. Different from per stirpes ("by branch"), where inheritance is divided by family branch first.</p>
  </div>
</div>

  <h3>üí° Did you know?</h3>
  <p>Most people intuitively prefer per stirpes over per capita for family distributions. Per capita can create surprising results‚Äîif you leave money "to my grandchildren per capita" and one child has one grandchild while another has five, the single grandchild gets the same as each of the five, making their branch receive much more overall.</p>
