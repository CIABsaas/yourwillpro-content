<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>A person or entity that assumes liability for another's debt, default, or failure to perform a legal obligation. In estate law, a surety often provides a bond guaranteeing an executor or administrator will properly perform their duties.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>A surety is someone who promises to pay up or perform if you don't. In estate matters, courts sometimes require executors to get a surety bond‚Äîbasically insurance that guarantees they won't steal or mismanage the estate.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="example-box">

  <p>"The court appointed my cousin as executor of Dad's $500,000 estate, but required him to get a surety bond for $500,000. If he steals or loses money, the surety company pays us back and then goes after my cousin to recover the loss."</p>
