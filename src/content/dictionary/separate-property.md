<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>Property owned by one spouse individually, acquired before marriage or during marriage by gift or inheritance, or designated as separate by prenuptial or postnuptial agreement. In common law jurisdictions, each spouse owns their separate property individually, while in community property jurisdictions, separate property is distinct from community property acquired during marriage.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>Separate property is what you own individually, not jointly with your spouse. Property you owned before marriage is separate property. Gifts and inheritances you receive (even during marriage) are usually separate property. Everything else acquired during marriage might be marital or community property, depending on where you live.</p>
  </div>
</div>
