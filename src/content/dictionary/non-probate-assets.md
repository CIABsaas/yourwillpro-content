<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>Property that passes directly to designated beneficiaries or joint owners upon death without going through probate. Common examples include jointly-owned property with right of survivorship, life insurance with named beneficiaries, retirement accounts, and assets held in living trusts. Not controlled by the will.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>Assets that automatically go to someone when you die, without going through probate or your will. Common examples: jointly-owned homes (go to surviving owner), life insurance (goes to named beneficiary), superannuation/retirement accounts with beneficiary designations, bank accounts with payable-on-death beneficiaries. These bypass your executor and transfer directly. Your will doesn't control them‚Äîtheir own beneficiary designations or ownership structure does.</p>
  </div>
</div>
