<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>A trust that may be altered, amended, or revoked entirely by the settlor during their lifetime while they retain capacity. Also called a living trust or inter vivos trust. The settlor typically serves as trustee and retains complete control over trust assets until death or incapacity, at which point the trust becomes irrevocable.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>A revocable trust is a trust you can change or cancel anytime while you're alive. You keep complete control‚Äîyou can add or remove assets, change beneficiaries, or dissolve the trust entirely. It's flexible and lets you maintain control while potentially avoiding probate when you die.</p>
