<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>A federal tax credit applied against both estate and gift taxes, effectively exempting a specified amount of transferred wealth from taxation. The unified credit corresponds to the basic exclusion amount (exemption), allowing individuals to transfer significant wealth during life or at death without federal estate or gift tax liability.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>The unified credit is the amount you can give away during life or leave when you die without paying federal estate or gift taxes. As of recent years, it's in the millions per person, so most estates don't owe federal estate tax.</p>
  </div>
</div>
