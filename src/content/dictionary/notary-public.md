<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>A person officially authorized by government to witness document signings, verify identity, administer oaths, and certify copies. Role and powers vary significantly: in common law countries, notaries handle specialized/international documents. In the US, widely used for routine witnessing. In civil law jurisdictions, highly trained legal professionals.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>Someone officially authorized to witness you signing documents and verify your identity. In the US, notaries are common and inexpensive, used for powers of attorney and many documents. In Australia and UK, notaries are less common‚Äîmainly for international documents or specialized transactions. Most wills don't need notarization in common law countries‚Äîordinary witnesses suffice. Notarization confirms who signed, not whether the document is legally valid.</p>
