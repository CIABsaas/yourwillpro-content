<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>A transfer of property that takes effect at the transferor's death rather than during life. Testamentary dispositions are typically made through wills and must comply with statutory formalities for wills, including proper execution and witnesses.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>A testamentary disposition is when you give something away in your will that the recipient only gets after you die. It's different from a lifetime gift‚Äîthis gift doesn't happen until you're gone.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="example-box">
  <div class="example-label">EXAMPLE</div>
  <p>"Dad wrote in a letter 'I want my car to go to my son when I die.' That letter wasn't witnessed or properly signed, so it failed as a testamentary disposition. Without proper will formalities, the car went through intestacy with the rest of his estate."</p>
