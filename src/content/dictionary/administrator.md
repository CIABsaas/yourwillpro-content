<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>A person appointed by the court to administer the estate of a deceased person who died intestate or where no executor is named or able to act.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>When someone dies without a will, the court picks someone to sort out their money and belongings. That person is the administrator.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="when-box">
  <h3>‚è± When you'll encounter this term</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>A family member died without leaving a will</li>
    <li>The named executor cannot or will not act</li>
    <li>Applying for Letters of Administration at the court</li>
    <li>Dealing with banks or property after a death</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div class="example-box">
  <div class="example-label">EXAMPLE</div>
  <p>"After Dad passed away without a will, my sister applied to become the administrator of his estate."</p>
</div>

<div class="why-matters">
  <h3>üí° Why this matters</h3>
  <p>If someone dies without a will, their estate can't be accessed until the court appoints an administrator. This process takes time ‚Äî often 4 to 8 weeks ‚Äî during which bank accounts are frozen and property can't be sold.</p>
  <p>Knowing who can apply, and the priority order, helps families act quickly and avoid costly delays.</p>
</div>
