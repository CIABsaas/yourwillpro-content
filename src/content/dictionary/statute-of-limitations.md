<div class="two-column">
  <div class="column-card legal-column">
    <div class="column-label">LEGAL DEFINITION</div>
    <p>Laws establishing maximum time periods within which legal proceedings must be commenced, varying by type of claim and jurisdiction. In estate matters, statutes of limitations govern when will contests must be filed, when family provision claims can be brought, when creditors must file claims against estates, and other time-sensitive estate proceedings.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="column-card plain-column">
    <div class="column-label">PLAIN ENGLISH</div>
    <p>Statutes of limitations are deadlines for filing lawsuits or claims. If you want to challenge a will or make a family provision claim, you typically have a limited time (often 6 months to a few years, depending on the claim type and jurisdiction). Miss the deadline and you lose your right to sue.</p>
  </div>
</div>
